# SqncR Vision

Why this exists. What it's trying to be.

---

## The Problem

You're coding. You want music. But:

- Spotify is passive (you're just listening)
- Opening a DAW breaks your flow
- Preset patterns get boring
- You want something that evolves

---

## The Vision

**Left monitor:** VSCode with code
**Right monitor:** Claude Desktop
**Your studio:** MIDI hardware ready

You type:
```
"create an ambient drone, 87 BPM, darker"
```

Your hardware synths start playing. Music you didn't program. Music that responds to what you ask for.

You keep coding. Occasionally:
```
"add some polyrhythms"
"make it brighter"
"save this"
```

The music evolves while you work.

---

## Core Principles

### 1. AI-Native
Natural language is the interface. Not a UI. Not a piano roll. You describe what you want.

### 2. Device-Agnostic
Works with what you own. Polyend, Moog, Korg, software synths, whatever. Add a device profile, it works.

### 3. Musically Intelligent
Not just "play notes." Real music theory:
- Scales and modes
- Voice leading
- Tension and resolution
- Groove and humanization

### 4. Conversation-Driven
Works inside Claude, Copilot, or any MCP-compatible assistant. The AI understands context across your conversation.

---

## What SqncR Is

- A MIDI generation engine
- An MCP server for AI assistants
- A CLI for direct control
- A bridge between intent and sound

## What SqncR Is Not

- A DAW (no recording, mixing, mastering)
- An audio engine (just MIDI)
- A replacement for learning music (a tool, not a teacher)
- A cloud service (runs locally, your hardware)

---

## Target Users

**Technical musicians who:**
- Code with AI assistants all day
- Have MIDI gear ready to go
- Think in APIs and real-time systems
- Want generative music while working

**If you're not technical:** SqncR might not be for you yet. It's a tool for people comfortable with terminals, configuration files, and MIDI routing.

---

## Why Build This?

Because no one has.

There are:
- AI music generators (but they output audio files, not MIDI)
- MIDI sequencers (but they don't understand natural language)
- MCP servers for coding tools (but none for music)

SqncR sits at the intersection:
**AI ∩ MIDI ∩ Natural Language ∩ Real Hardware**

---

## The Name

**SqncR** = Sequencer

Pronounced "sequencer" (the 'u' and 'e' are silent, like the future).
